About
Grandma's Mama's Recipes

Grandma's Mama's Recipes is a cookbook written by my grandfather, Karrel Ardoin, around the mid 90's. It is a collection of recipes from his friends and family alongside a few personal stories about life in Louisiana's Cajun Country in the early 1900's.

From the back of the book:

Souvenir of Cajun Country

"Grandma's Mama's Recipes"

"Cajun Cooking is what you eat at your Mama's house or your Grandma's house." Cajun Cooking is Easy & Fast.
In those days, Cajun ladies worked in the fields. The lady of the house left the field about 30-minutes before the men--to fix dinner for the group. She went back to work when they did.

FREE BENEFITS! You get the picture, lifestory, and favorite recipes of:
Newest Governor of Louisiana --- Mike Foster
Oldest living Govenor (93) -- Jimmie Davis
Only Govenor to serve 4 terms -- Edwin Edwards
Louisiana's Most Notorious Govenor -- Huey P. Long
Of Grand Ole Opry fame -- Jimmy C. Newman

Goldie's "Black Eye Pea Cake" recipe is over 100 years old. Goldie, 87, ate cakes her mother made from that same recipe. It's delicious.

Bernadette is 87 years old yet her syrup pies are simply scrupmtious. Syrip pies were a staple. Syrup was plentiful; every farmer had a sugar cane patch.

Malou's candy is so good, it's immoral. When you see company coming, it makes you want to hide it under a bed-- to keep it all for yourself.

Anita Fontenot won the 1996 FNBC Recipe Contest with her "Crawfish Bisque" recipe.

"Ta-Ta" Guillory won several awards from food companies with her "Tartar Sauce" recipe.

The recipes are old -- only the very best survived the test of time. This book will make you proud. Your cooking will taste like Real Cajun Cooking because it will be real Cajun Cooking. You'll use & enjoy this book for years to come.
